About the Program

This STEM education initiative is a collaboration between Ethiopian Airlines, Boeing, and Think Young. It brings together top-performing Grade 11 students from across Ethiopia to inspire future innovators in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

Each region is represented by one male and one female student who have demonstrated outstanding academic achievement and leadership potential.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who can participate in the STEM program?

Grade 11 students from all Ethiopian regions who excel academically and show strong interest in STEM subjects. One male and one female are selected per region.

2. How are students selected?

The selection is done in collaboration with regional education bureaus. Candidates are chosen based on academic performance, STEM-related achievements, and leadership qualities.

3. What organizations are behind the program?

The program is jointly organized by Ethiopian Airlines, Boeing, and Think Young, combining aviation industry expertise with educational innovation.

4. Where does the program take place?

The main sessions and workshops are hosted at Ethiopian Airlines facilities and other selected venues equipped for STEM activities.

5. What will students learn?

Students will explore aviation, engineering, coding, robotics, and problem-solving skills through hands-on projects, expert talks, and collaborative challenges.

6. Is there a cost to participate?

No. The program is fully funded by Ethiopian Airlines, Boeing, and Think Young, including transportation, meals, and learning materials.

7. Will participants receive a certificate?

Yes. All participants who successfully complete the program will receive a certificate recognizing their achievement and participation.

8. How long does the program last?

The STEM camp runs for several days, with a structured daily schedule of workshops, activities, and guest speaker sessions.

9. Can students apply directly?

Students cannot apply directly; they must be nominated by their school or regional education office.

10. What should participants bring?

Bring personal items, comfortable clothing, and an open mind ready to learn, collaborate, and innovate.
